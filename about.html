MEHAR CHAND MAHAJAN DAV COLLEGE, KANGRA (H.P)

Maharishi Dayanand Saraswati ,a visionary and a social reformer, Considered education to be the only means to achieve freedom The real foundation of modern India was actually laid down by Swami Dayanand. The great ascetic found that without imparting education it was impossible to revive the past glory of India, so he founded the Arya Samaj in Mumbai on 10 April, 1875. After the demise of this great visionary. a group of his followers decided to start an educational institution to fulfil the dream long cherished by Swamiji. The establishment of Dayanand Anglo Vedic College Trust and Management marked the e The guiding principle of this august body is beginning of DAV Movement in the field of ieducation. to provide quality education at affordable cost to all, especially those who needed it the most-- the women, the weaker and the educationally backward sections of the society MCM DAV College, Kangra is one of the hundreds of such institutions being run across India and abroad to impart quality education imbued with nationalism and endeavouring to impart t scientific education to both the boys and the girls. With the untiring efforts of Sh. CD Khanna and Sh. DK Mahajan, the DAV College Managing Committee started MCM DAV College, Kangra. Nestled in the verdant Kangra valley at the foothills of the majestic Dhauladhars, MCM DAV College, Kangra was founded in 1976. Since its inception the College has redeemed its pledge of providing quality education along with moral values to students.

Degree courses B.A., B.Sc., B.Com and BCA.

A wide array of undergraduate courses such as Bachelor of Arts (BA), Bachelor of Science (B.Sc), Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com), and Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA) are commonly available in many colleges across.
The specific availability of these courses can vary depending on the college's specializations, affiliations, and infrastructure.

General Availability and Key Features:

Bachelor of Arts (BA):
This is a widely offered undergraduate degree with a duration of three years. It provides flexibility for students to specialize in various subjects within humanities, social sciences, and arts. Common specializations include English, History, Political Science, Economics, Psychology, Sociology, and many regional languages and literature. BA programs aim to develop critical thinking, communication, and analytical skills.
Bachelor of Science (B.Sc):
Another popular three-year undergraduate degree focusing on science and technology-related subjects. It offers specializations like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology (Botany, Zoology), Computer Science, Information Technology, Agriculture, Nursing, and more. B.Sc programs emphasize practical knowledge through laboratory work and aim to build a strong foundation in scientific principles.
Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com):
A three-year undergraduate program centered around commerce, finance, accounting, and business-related subjects. It equips students with knowledge in areas like accounting, economics, business law, taxation, and financial management. B.Com is a common choice for students seeking careers in finance, banking, and business administration. Some colleges also offer specialized B.Com (Honours) programs with a more in-depth focus.
Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA):
This is a three-year professional undergraduate degree focused on computer science and its applications. The curriculum typically covers programming languages, database management, web development, software engineering, and computer networks. BCA programs aim to prepare students for careers in the IT industry.

Factors Affecting Availability:

Type of College:
Arts and Science colleges usually offer BA and B.Sc programs. Commerce colleges primarily focus on B.Com. Colleges specializing in technology and computer studies will likely offer BCA. Comprehensive universities often have multiple departments and offer all four courses.
Affiliation:
affiliated with different universities might have varying course offerings based on the university's curriculum and guidelines.
Location:
availability of specific courses can also be influenced by the region and the demand for particular programs in that area.
Infrastructure and Resources: and BCA programs often require specific laboratory facilities and computer equipment, which might affect their availability in all colleges.

Admission Process:

Admission to these undergraduate courses is generally based on the marks obtained in the 10+2 (or equivalent) examination. Some colleges and universities might also conduct entrance examinations for specific programs or have minimum percentage requirements. The admission process typically involves filling out an application form, submitting necessary documents, and attending counseling or appearing for entrance tests if applicable. It is recommended to check the specific websites or admission brochures of individual colleges to get accurate and up-to-date information about the availability of BA, B.Sc, B.Com, and BCA courses. You can also inquire directly with the admissions offices of the colleges you are interested in.
